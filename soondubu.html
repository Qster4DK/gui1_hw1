Soondubu (Korean Tofu Soup)

Soondubu jjigae is a spicy Korean soup featuring soft tofu as a main protein. It is filled with vegetables and can even have meat added to it for a boost of protein.

It is perfect for a cold day and can be made very easily with any leftovers in the fridge. It is best served with jasmine rice and korean side dishes such as kimchi or pickled radish.

Ingredients

  • Soondubu, or Korean soft tofu
  • Water or low-sodium chicken broth, vegetable broth, dashi, or Korean anchovy stock for more flavor
  • Sesame oil
  • Vegetable oil
  • Onion
  • Scallions
  • Gochugaru (Korean chili flakes)
  • Sugar
  • Garlic
  • Soy sauce
  • Oyster sauce
  • Any vegetables such as zucchini or mushrooms
  • Additional protein such as pork, dumplings, or fish balls

Instructions

  • Heat your pot on medium low heat and add your gochugaru, garlic, sugar, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and vegetable oil.
  • Add white parts of your scallion and onion and cook until fragrant.
  • If using meat, add it in now to stir fry it in the sauce.
  • Add water or stock. Once it comes to a boil, add your vegetables.
  • Add your soft toft with a spoon. Be careful not to break it up too much.
  • Season to taste and add the green part of your scallions to garnish. Feel free to add in an egg to soft boil.
